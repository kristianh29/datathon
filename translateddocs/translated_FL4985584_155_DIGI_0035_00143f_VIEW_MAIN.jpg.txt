REGULATION concerning raw flax, processed flax, and tow (bark).

Annulment of the regulations of December 1, 1915 (Regulations Gazette for the Operational Area of the 4th Army No. 15, pp. 122-123), April 15, 1916 (Regulations Gazette for the Operational Area of the 4th Army, No. 32, pp. 232-234), and August 9, 1916 (Regulations Gazette for the Operational Area of the 4th Army No. 46, pp. 360-361). The following applies to the area of the 4th Army concerning raw flax, processed flax, and tow (bark):

ARTICLE 1. — Whoever holds stocks of raw flax, processed flax, or tow (bark) in storage must report the quantities by the 3rd of each month to the council (mayor and aldermen) of the municipality where the storage place is located, separated by raw flax, processed flax, and tow, including the owners and the place of storage.

The municipalities, through the mayors and aldermen, must report by the 5th of each month the stocks of raw flax, processed flax, and tow present in their vicinity, including the owners and the place of storage, to the Stage or Place Commanders.

ARTICLE 2. — The following maximum prices are set for processed flax and tow (bark) from old and new harvests:
1) Water-retted flax: a) 250,— to 350,— francs, depending on quality, per bale of 104 kilos gross or 103 kilos net of Kortrijk flax.

b) 190,— to 250,— francs, depending on quality, for 100 kilos of Flemish blue flax.
2) Grass-retted flax (field retting): 130,— to 180,— francs, depending on quality, for 100 kilos of scutched grass-retted flax, regardless of origin.

3) Tow (Bark): a) 80,— francs for best quality dressed (reprocessed) tow.
b) 100,— francs for pure nature of best quality.

c) 120,— francs for best quality ends.
d) 140,— francs for combings (heckled) of best quality.
For raw flax, the price corresponds to the set highest prices for processed (scutched) flax.

For lesser quality and poorer processing, the highest prices decrease accordingly.
ARTICLE 3. — Any transport of raw flax, raw hemp, processed flax, or tow (bark) without a permit from the Stage Inspection (Economic Committee) or the flax offices at Kortrijk and Lokeren is prohibited.

The sale of processed flax or tow is allowed only to:

1) The raw materials department ("Rohstoff-Abteilung") in Ghent, Gouvernementstraat 18.
2) The flax offices at Kortrijk and Lokeren.

The sale of raw flax for processing is permitted with approval from the "Wirtschaftsausschuss" or the flax offices at Kortrijk and Lokeren to the flax processing establishments.

ARTICLE 4. — Stocks of raw flax, processed flax, and tow from old and new harvests must be surrendered on demand of the authorized Stage or Place Commandant to the official offices identified in Article 3 under 1 and 2. If there is no agreement on the sale price, the price will be set by the administrations mentioned in Article 3 under 1 and 2, together with a Belgian expert.

ARTICLE 5. — Punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to 30,000 marks or one of these penalties:

1) Those who do not, do not precisely, or incorrectly report their stocks to the municipal authorities.

Mayors and aldermen who send incorrect reports about stocks to the Stage or Place Commandant, which they knew or should have known to be incorrect, will face the same penalties.

2) Those who exceed the set maximum prices.
3) Those who invite another person to conclude a contract which exceeds the maximum prices, or who offer themselves for such a contract.

4) Those who transport raw flax, processed flax, or tow without permission.
5) Those who sell or otherwise divest raw flax, processed flax, or tow to others than the authorities specified in Article 3 Items 1 and 2, or without permission to flax processing establishments.

6) Those who, despite the order of the competent Stage or Place Commandant, do not deliver their stocks of flax, tow, and raw flax to the authorities specified in Article 3 under 1 and 2.

7) Those who destroy or make unusable the stocks of raw flax, processed flax, or tow.
The stocks involved in the violation are to be confiscated.
The German military courts and military authorities have jurisdiction.

ARTICLE 6. This regulation comes into effect the day it is proclaimed.

A. H. Q., December 23, 1916 The Supreme Commander, Duke ALBRECHT OF WÜRTTEMBERG.

REGULATION concerning the inventory and usage of osiers.

1. — The sale and processing of all dry, green, and peeled osiers of any length in the area of the Fourth Army without a permit from the Stage Inspection 4 (Economic Committee) is hereby prohibited.

2. — Anyone storing stocks of dry, green, or peeled osiers must declare the available stocks, indicating the owner and place of storage, to their municipal government by January 10, 1917; this must send the declarations to the Kommendanture by January 15, 1917. The Kommendanture must submit these collated declarations to the Stage Inspection 4 (Economic Committee) by January 20, 1917:

The declarations must specify: a) How many bundles of osiers over 1.80 m in length, sorted by length.
b) How many bundles of unsorted osiers, divided into green and peeled, from a previous harvest are present.
Anyone possessing new harvest osiers must declare the stocks in the same manner, separated into already cut bundles and not yet cut by cultivated area in hectares.

3. — The price is determined as:
For bundles of sorted by length green osiers fr. 2.
For bundles of unsorted by length green osiers fr. 1.50

For bundles of peeled osiers from fr. 3.60 to 4.20
Bundles that, upon surrender, do not have the prescribed dimension of 1 m will have 20 centimes deducted for each centimeter lacking.

4. — Boiling and peeling dry and green osiers without a permit from the Stage Inspection 4 (Economic Committee) is prohibited.

5. — It is commanded hereby to cut this year’s osiers in time. Osiers growers are responsible for the proper care and handling of their osiers. If it is impossible to cut them immediately because of high water levels, this must be done immediately after the water levels fall.

6. — A fine up to 10,000 marks and imprisonment for up to 2 years or one of these penalties — unless stricter penalties are applicable under existing laws and regulations — will be applied:
1.) To those who violate provisions under items 1, 3, 4, and 5.
2.) To those who fail to declare, incorrectly declare, or do not declare their stocks promptly.
Additionally, the objects related to the offense will be confiscated.

7. — The German military courts and military authorities have jurisdiction.

A. H. Q., December 28, 1916. The Supreme Commander, Duke ALBRECHT OF WÜRTTEMBERG.
