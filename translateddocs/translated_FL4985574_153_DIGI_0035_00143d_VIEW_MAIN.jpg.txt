REGULATION concerning straw flax, processed flax, and tow (cloddens).

Repealing the regulations of 1 December 1915 (Regulatory Gazette for the Rear Area of the 4th Army No. 15, pp. 122-123), of 15 April 1916 (Regulatory Gazette for the Rear Area of the 4th Army, No. 32, pp. 232-234), and of 9 August 1916 (Regulatory Gazette for the Rear Area of the 4th Army No. 46, pp. 360-362), the following regulation is established for the area of the 4th Army concerning straw flax, processed flax, and tow (cloddens):

ARTICLE 1. — Whoever has stocks of straw flax, processed flax, or tow (cloddens) in storage is obliged to report the quantities, separated by straw flax, processed flax, and cloddens, and with the designation of the owners and the storage place, to the administration (mayor and aldermen) of the municipality on whose territory the storage place is located by the 3rd of each month.

The municipalities must report the available stocks of straw flax, processed flax, and cloddens in their vicinity, with the designation of the owners and the storage places, to the Step or Local Command Offices by the 5th of each month.

ARTICLE 2. — For processed flax and tow (cloddens) from the old and new harvests, the following maximum prices are set:
1) For water-retted flax: a) 250,— to 350,— francs, depending on quality, per bale of 104 kilos gross or 103 kilos net of Kortrijk flax.
b) 190,— to 250,— francs, depending on quality, for 100 kilos of Flemish blue flax.
2) For dew-retted flax (Field-rotted): 130,— to 180,— francs, depending on quality, for 100 kilos scutched dew-retted flax, regardless of origin.
3) Cloddens (Remains): a) 80,— francs for scutched (retravaillées) of best grades.
b) 100,— francs for pure naturals of best quality.
c) 120,— francs for snippets of best quality.
d) 140,— francs for hackled ones (combed) of best quality.
For straw flax, the price corresponding to the determined maximum prices for processed (scutched) flax is used.

The maximum prices are reduced accordingly for lesser quality and inferior processing.

ARTICLE 3. — Transport of straw flax, raw hemp, processed flax, or tow (cloddens) without a permit from the Step Inspection (Wirtschaftsausschuss) or the flax offices in Kortrijk and Lokeren is prohibited.

The sale of processed flax or cloddens may only be conducted with:
1) The Department for Raw Materials (“Rohstoff-Abteilung”) in Ghent, Gouvernementstraat, 18.
2) The flax offices in Kortrijk and Lokeren.

The sale of straw flax intended for processing, with the permission of the "Wirtschaftsausschuss" or the flax offices in Kortrijk and Lokeren, may also be conducted with flax processing facilities.

ARTICLE 4. — Stocks of straw flax, processed flax, and cloddens from the old and new harvests must be surrendered on request to the competent Step or Local Command Offices to the official offices referred to in article 3 under 1 and 2. If no agreement is reached on the sales sum, the price will be determined by the authorities referred to in article 3 under 1 and 2, together with a Belgian expert.

ARTICLE 5. — Punishable with a maximum of 3 years imprisonment and a maximum fine of 30,000 marks, or with one of these penalties, are:
1) Whoever does not report their stocks to the municipal governments, does not report them in a timely manner or inaccurately.
With the same penalties, mayors and aldermen who have forwarded statements about stocks to the Step or Local Command Offices are punished if they knew or should have known that they are incorrect.
2) Whoever exceeds the determined maximum prices.
3) Whoever invites another person to conclude a contract by which the maximum prices are exceeded or offers themselves for such a contract.
4) Whoever transports straw flax, processed flax, or cloddens without permission.
5) Whoever sells or otherwise disposes of straw flax, processed flax, or cloddens to others than the authorities mentioned in article 3 under 1 and 2 or without permission to flax processing facilities.
6) Whoever, despite the order of the competent Step or Local Command Offices, does not deliver their stocks of flax, cloddens, and straw flax to the authorities mentioned in article 3 under 1 and 2.
7) Whoever destroys or makes unusable stocks of straw flax, processed flax, or cloddens.

The stocks to which the offense relates must be confiscated.

The German military courts and military authorities have jurisdiction.

ARTICLE 6. This regulation shall come into force the day after its proclamation.

A. H. Q., 23 December 1916 The Supreme Commander, Duke ALBRECHT OF WÜRTTEMBERG.
