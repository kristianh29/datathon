Regulation concerning flax straw, processed flax, and tow (clods).

Repealing the regulations of December 1, 1915 (Regulation Gazette for the stage area of the 4th Army No. 15, p. 122-123), April 15, 1916 (Regulation Gazette for the stage area of the 4th Army, No. 32, p. 232-234), and August 9, 1916 (Regulation Gazette for the stage area of the 4th Army No. 46, p. 360-362), it is decreed for the area of the 4th Army concerning flax straw, processed flax, and tow (clods) as follows:

ARTICLE 1. — Anyone storing stocks of flax straw, processed flax, or tow (clods) is required to report the quantities available at the end of each month, separated by flax straw, processed flax, and tow (clods), along with the owners and the storage location, to the authorities (mayor and aldermen) of the municipality where the storage location is situated, by the 3rd of each month.

Municipalities must report to the Etappe or Ortskommandanturen the stocks of flax straw, processed flax, and tow (clods) available in the vicinity, along with the owners and the storage location, by the 5th of each month.

ARTICLE 2. — The following maximum prices are set for processed flax and tow (clods) from the old and new harvests:
1) For water-retted flax: a) 250 to 350 francs, according to quality, per bale of 104 kilos gross or 103 kilos net of Courtrai flax.
b) 190 to 250 francs, according to quality, for 100 kilos of Flemish blue flax.
2) For dew-retted flax (field retting): 130 to 180 francs, according to quality, for 100 kilos scutched. from any origin.
3) Clods (Emouchures): a) 80 francs for the best quality scutched (retravaillées).
b) 100 francs for the best quality pure nature.
c) 120 francs for the best quality snouts.
d) 140 francs for the best quality hacklers (combed).
For flax straw, the price is calculated to correspond to the highest prices set for processed (scutched) flax.
For lower quality and poorer processing, the highest prices are reduced accordingly.

ARTICLE 3. — The transport of flax straw, raw hemp, processed flax, or tow (clods) without a permit from the Etappe Inspection (Economic Committee) or the flax offices in Courtrai and Lokeren is prohibited.

The sale of processed flax or clods may only occur to:
1) The department for raw materials (“Rohstoff-Abteilung”) in Gent, Gouvernementstraat 18.
2) The flax offices in Courtrai and Lokeren.

The sale of flax straw for the purpose of processing may, with the permission of the "Economic Committee" or the flax offices in Courtrai and Lokeren, also be made to flax processing establishments.

ARTICLE 4. — Stocks of flax straw, processed flax, and clods from the old and new harvests must be surrendered to the official offices indicated in article 3, 1 and 2, upon request by the competent Etappe or Ortskommandanturen. If no agreement is reached on the sale price, the price will be set by the administrations mentioned in article 3, 1 and 2, together with a Belgian expert.

ARTICLE 5. — Punishable with up to 3 years imprisonment and fines up to 30,000 marks, or with one of these penalties are:
1) Anyone who does not, not promptly, or inaccurately reports stocks to the municipal authorities.
With the same penalties, mayors and aldermen are punished who have sent inaccurate stock reports, of which they knew or should have known given the circumstances, to the Etappe or Ortskommandanturen.
2) Anyone who exceeds the set maximum prices.
3) Anyone inviting another person to conclude a contract that exceeds the maximum prices or offers themselves for such a contract.
4) Anyone transporting flax straw, processed flax, or clods without permission.
5) Anyone selling or otherwise disposing of flax straw, processed flax, or clods to others than the authorities mentioned in article 3, 1 and 2, or without permission to flax processing establishments.
6) Anyone who fails to deliver their stocks of flax, clods, and flax straw to the authorities as ordered by the competent Etappe or Ortskommandantur according to article 3, 1 and 2.
7) Anyone who destroys or renders useless stocks of flax straw, processed flax, or clods.

The stocks related to the violation must be confiscated.
Competent are German military courts and military administrations.

ARTICLE 6. This regulation becomes effective on the day of its announcement.

A. H. Q., December 23, 1916, The Supreme Commander, Duke ALBRECHT OF WÜRTTEMBERG.

Regulation concerning the inventory and use of willows.

1. — The sale and processing of all dry, green, and peeled willows of every length in the area of the stage without a permit from the Etappe Inspection 4 (Economic Committee) is hereby prohibited.

2. — Anyone having stocks of dry, green, or peeled willows in storage is required to report the available stocks, indicating the owner and the storage location, to their municipal administration by January 10, 1917. This must send the report to the Commandanture by January 15, 1917. The Commandantures must submit these collected reports to

...

January 20, 1917, to the Etappe Inspection 4 (Economic Committee).
The reports must show: a) How many bundles of willows over 1.80 m length, sorted by length.
b) How many bundles unsorted by length willows, separated into green and peeled willows from a previous harvest are available.
Anyone possessing willows from the new harvest must report the stocks in the same manner, and separately indicate those already cut in bundles, and those not yet cut by the cultivated area in hectares.

3. — The price is set as:
For sorted by length green willows fr. 2.—
For unsorted by length green willows fr. 1.50

For peeled willows from fr. 3.60 to 4.20
- For bundles that do not have the prescribed circumference of | m upon delivery, 20 centimes are deducted for each centimeter short.

4. — The cooking and peeling of dry and green willows without a permit from the Etappe Inspection 4 (Economic Committee) is forbidden.

5. — It is ordered that the willows of this year are to be cut in time. The owners of willow plants are responsible for proper care and handling of their willows.
If it is impossible to cut them immediately due to the high water level, this must be done immediately after the water recedes.

6. — Punishable with fines up to 10,000 marks and imprisonment up to 2 years, or with one of these penalties — unless stricter penalties apply according to existing laws and regulations — is:
1.) Anyone who violates provisions under 1, 3, 4, and 5.
2.) Anyone who does not, not accurately, or not on time reports their stocks.

In addition, the objects to which the violation relates are to be confiscated.

7. — Competent are German military courts and military administrations.
